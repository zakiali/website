Zaki Ali

I'm a 4th year graduate student in astrophysics at the University of California, Berkeley (UCB). I grew up in the great state of California and have been working my way up the coast through the years. I completed my undergraduate education in Mathematics and Astronomy from UCB in 2010. Following that, I spent two years working as a Junior Specialist engineer in the Radio Astronomy Lab at UCB. During my time with RAL, I built digital backends for the Precision Array for Probing the Epoch of Reionization (PAPER). I started graduate school at UCB in 2012 and am working with Professor Aaron Parsons on PAPER and the Hydrogen Epoch of Reionization Array (HERA) trying to find the signautre of when the first stars and galaxies formed in the universe.
